Part A – Case Study

Title: UPI Payment Fraud through Fake Payment Request

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime where scammers trick users into sending money or revealing sensitive details. One realistic scenario involves a college student selling a used phone online. The scam usually begins when a fraudster contacts the seller pretending to be a genuine buyer. They agree to purchase the item and send a fake payment request via a UPI app (like Google Pay or PhonePe). The scammer tells the victim that they need to “accept” the request to receive money. However, instead of receiving money, the victim unknowingly authorizes a payment and transfers money to the scammer. In some cases, the fraudster may also send fake screenshots showing successful payment or ask for OTPs under the pretext of “verifying the transaction.” Once the victim shares the OTP or approves the request, money is instantly debited from their account. Students, small sellers, and people unfamiliar with digital payments are the most common targets. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and sometimes leakage of personal banking information. This type of fraud spreads because of lack of awareness about how UPI actually works—you never need to enter a PIN to receive money. Understanding this basic rule can prevent many such scams.
